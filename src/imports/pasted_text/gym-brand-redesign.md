Redesign this homepage into a premium, modern, dark-mode gym brand with a raw old-school British identity.

Brand essence:
Brits Brothers is not a commercial gym brand and not an influencer fitness brand. It should feel like a serious British strength culture brand built around lifting, getting bigger, training properly, discipline, and standards. The vibe should feel masculine, rugged, premium, heritage-driven, and slightly intimidating. It should feel like an elite old-school training environment with real grit and authority.

Core personality:

* Masculine
* Angular
* Thick
* Disciplined
* Serious
* Gritty
* Premium but rugged
* Heritage-led
* No-nonsense
* Slightly intimidating
* British identity without feeling cheesy or costume-like

Creative direction:
The brand should combine:

* black and white photography
* dark-mode editorial design
* strong typography
* old-school British lifting culture
* distressed ripped and torn styling
* minimal, strategic use of the brand colors

The site should feel like:

* a serious iron house
* a premium underground strength brand
* modern in layout, but raw in attitude
* clean in structure, distressed in the details

Brand colors:
Use these brand colors exactly:

* Off-white: #fdfdff
* Deep royal blue: #24186e
* Strong red: #cc1e23

Use these environmental dark neutrals:

* Primary charcoal background: #121214
* Elevated charcoal surface: #1c1c1e

Supporting text and UI tones:

* Use grey body text that reads well on dark backgrounds
* Use muted greys for borders, dividers, and supporting UI
* Keep contrast strong so white can act as the sharpest callout color

Color strategy:
Use color sparingly and intentionally. Most of the experience should feel restrained and dark, letting the color moments hit harder.

Assign these specific jobs to the colors:

White:

* primary calls to action
* main conversion buttons
* key callout moments
* the most important action on a page
* should feel like the highest-contrast action color in the dark environment

Red:

* urgency
* intensity
* commitment
* event-related content
* event promotions
* high-pressure call-to-action moments
* should also serve as the dedicated color for an Events area because the brand will be promoting many events

Blue:

* supporting brand identity
* British heritage accent
* navigation states
* secondary accents
* structural brand moments
* subtle overlays
* section accents or labels
* should be used carefully, not as the dominant action color

Important note:
Do not overuse the red, blue, or white. The site should feel controlled, premium, and deliberate, not loud.

Photography direction:
Photography should be mostly black and white by default.
Use high-contrast, gritty, dramatic photography focused on:

* lifting
* heavy weights
* old-school gym culture
* sweat
* chalk
* plates
* tape
* grit
* texture
* masculine training environments

Avoid glossy commercial fitness photography and avoid generic stock gym imagery.

Image interaction styling:
In selected areas of the site, design image rollovers where black and white images reveal full color on hover.
This should feel premium and dramatic, not flashy.
Use this effect on areas like:

* coach cards
* event cards
* gallery images
* transformation/results content
* featured service blocks

The point is to let color feel earned through interaction rather than always present.

Typography:
Use a thick, blocky, angular, masculine display font for:

* hero headlines
* section titles
* pricing headers
* navigation emphasis
* stat callouts
* CTA text

Pair that with an Old English display font used sparingly for:

* short heritage accents
* section overlines
* brand stamps
* small graphic phrases
* identity moments
* badge-like brand features

Do not use the Old English font for long text blocks, buttons, or paragraphs.

Use a clean sans serif like Work Sans for body copy.
Use medium to semi-bold weight because the body copy will sit in grey on dark backgrounds and still needs to feel strong and readable.

Typography tone:

* bold
* blocky
* masculine
* angular
* disciplined
* premium
* heritage-aware
* clean and modern in structure

Section divider styling:
A key brand signature should be distressed torn section borders between content blocks.

The site should use ripped, torn, splatter-like section edges that feel like:

* ripped poster layers
* distressed paper tears
* battle-worn textures
* torn print edges
* splatter-cut transitions

These torn edges should divide sections in a dramatic but controlled way.
They should not look like soft wave dividers or generic website separators.
They should feel sharp, raw, irregular, and brand-specific.

Important implementation note for the mockup:
Design these torn edges visually in the comps as signature dividers that can later be translated into CSS masking, SVG masks, or custom Divi section dividers by the developer.
The design should show the intended look clearly, even though the final website implementation may use CSS object masking or pseudo-elements.

Layout goals:

* strong visual hierarchy
* bold, simple sections
* clear pacing
* clean composition with distressed details
* premium spacing
* mobile-first
* conversion-focused
* serious, masculine visual rhythm
* dramatic but restrained use of texture and color

The design should feel expensive through restraint, structure, contrast, and attitude.

Homepage structure:
Include these sections in a premium, conversion-focused flow:

1. Hero section

* bold statement headline
* short no-nonsense subheadline
* strong primary CTA
* secondary CTA
* black and white hero image or background treatment
* immediate sense of strength, identity, and seriousness

2. Brand identity / philosophy section

* explain the old-school British lifting standard
* frame the brand as a training code, not a trend
* communicate discipline, standards, strength, and serious intent

3. Services / outcomes section

* size
* strength
* coaching
* accountability
* or similar structured outcomes
* make the offer feel clear and strong, not cluttered

4. Coach section for Olly

* position Olly as the authority
* experienced, old-school, serious, and direct
* no influencer energy
* no fluff
* should feel like a real coach with standards

5. Events section

* this is important and should have a dedicated area
* events are a key part of the brand and will be promoted often
* use red as the defining accent color for this section and related event calls to action
* the section should feel energetic, urgent, and culturally important to the brand
* event cards or promos should feel bold and dramatic

6. Testimonials / results section

* social proof
* real people
* earned results
* clean and credible presentation
* can use black and white imagery with color hover reveals

7. Pricing / coaching options section

* clean cards or structured tiers
* simple, premium presentation
* no clutter
* conversion-focused

8. Final CTA section

* forceful closing statement
* one strong action
* serious brand sign-off
* should feel like a final challenge or invitation into the standard

Tone of voice:

* direct
* confident
* disciplined
* slightly intimidating
* serious
* no corporate language
* no generic fitness phrases
* no wellness clichés
* no soft motivational language

Words and phrases that fit the tone:

* standard
* earned
* built
* forged
* trained properly
* serious only
* under the banner
* no gimmicks
* no shortcuts
* old-school strength
* discipline
* code
* brotherhood

Visual balance:
The design should mostly live in dark charcoal and black and white photography.
Red and white should stand out more than blue.
Blue should be present as a grounding heritage color, but used sparingly.
White should be the sharpest high-contrast action color.
Red should own urgency and events.

Goal:
Create a homepage that feels like an elite old-school British strength brand with premium design, dark-mode editorial structure, black and white photography, strategic color discipline, distressed torn section edges, and a powerful masculine presence.
